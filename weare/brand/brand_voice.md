# CHATTY
## brand voice & tone guidelines
*the complete content playbook for every channel*

> "We create sales, not support."

Chatty is the AI-first chat platform built for eCommerce sales — not customer service. While others race to deflect tickets faster, we focus on one thing: turning every conversation into revenue.

our position in one line:

**"The AI-first chat platform that sells for you."**

Every product question is someone silently asking: "should I buy this?" Most stores waste that moment. Chatty captures it.

## 2. brand personality

If Chatty were a person, they'd be the sales expert on your team who also happens to be ridiculously good at tech — someone who gets to the point, knows their stuff, and never wastes your time.

### four core traits

| trait | what it means | what it sounds like |
|---|---|---|
| bold & direct | we say the uncomfortable thing others avoid. no corporate fluff. | "Most chat apps are broken. Here's why." |
| confident but grounded | we back every claim with data. no empty hype. | "Decathlon made €1,078 in 7 days — not from support, from smart selling." |
| clever, not cute | wit is a spice, not the whole dish. we're smart, occasionally funny, never silly. | "Your chatbot answers questions. Chatty closes deals." |
| human & direct | we talk to merchants like a knowledgeable friend — straight talk, no condescension. | "You don't need another help desk. You need a salesperson." |

---

## 3. voice vs. tone — what's the difference

Our voice is who we are — it never changes. Our tone is how we express that voice in context — it adapts.

| voice (always consistent) | tone (adapts to context) |
|---|---|
| bold & sales-first | energetic in marketing & social |
| data-backed & credible | authoritative in blogs & thought leadership |
| direct & no-fluff | clear & instructional in user guides |
| human & approachable | calm & supportive in error/support content |

## 4. tone by channel

### 4.1 marketing & promotional content

**context:** ads, social posts, app store listing, email campaigns, homepage copy  
**energy level:** high. we're selling an idea, not explaining a feature.

- lead with the problem or the provocative truth
- use short, punchy sentences. one idea per line.
- numbers beat adjectives. say "€1,078 in 7 days" not "incredible results"
- wit is welcome — one sharp line per piece, not a comedy show
- always end with a clear action

| | write this | not this |
|---|---|---|
| hook | ✅ 90% of chat apps are broken. here's what they're costing you. | ❌ Chatty is a revolutionary AI platform that transforms customer engagement. |
| CTA | ✅ see how much you're leaving on the table. try Chatty free. | ❌ click here to learn more about our solution. |
| social | ✅ your chatbot answers questions. Chatty closes deals. | ❌ Chatty is the best AI chatbot for Shopify merchants everywhere! |

---

### 4.2 blog & thought leadership

**context:** blog posts, CEO articles, LinkedIn long-form, industry commentary  
**energy level:** medium-high. authoritative and contrarian. we challenge the industry's assumptions — and back it up.

- open with a provocative statement or uncomfortable truth
- show, don't tell — use real data (Decathlon, 2,000+ merchants, etc.)
- position Chatty as a category creator, not a feature improvement
- challenge the metrics the industry celebrates — deflection rates, response time — and reframe them
- use "others do X, we do Y" structure
- end with a clear, confident CTA — not a hedge

> **tone in one phrase:** "the smartest person in the room who actually wants to share what they know."

---

### 4.3 in-app content & UX writing

**context:** tooltips, empty states, labels, buttons, confirmations, error messages  
**energy level:** low. calm, clear, helpful. get the user to the next step — fast.

- lead with what the user needs to do, not what the feature does
- active voice, always. "Chatty found 3 unresolved questions" not "3 questions were found"
- labels describe the action: "add data source" not "add"
- never inject personality into errors or negative states
- avoid "please" — it gets overused and loses meaning
- use contractions (it's, you've, can't) — they sound more human

| context | write this | not this |
|---|---|---|
| empty state | ✅ no data sources yet. add your first one to start training the AI. | ❌ there is currently no data available in this section. |
| error | ✅ something went wrong. try again or contact support. | ❌ uh oh! oopsie — something broke! 😅 |
| button | ✅ train AI | ❌ click here |
| success | ✅ AI training complete. test it now to see how it responds. | ❌ awesome! training done! 🎉 let's gooo! |
| tooltip | ✅ tone sets how the AI sounds to your customers — friendly, professional, or empathetic. | ❌ this is the tone setting for your AI assistant's voice configuration. |

---

### 4.4 user guide & help documentation

**context:** help center articles, onboarding guides, troubleshooting docs  
**energy level:** low-medium. task-oriented, instructional, never dry.

- open with context: what this is, who it's for, why they'd care
- follow 5W1H structure: what, who, why, when, where, how
- write from the user's pain point, not from the feature's capabilities
- start with lighter, contextual tone — shift to direct, instructional tone for steps
- use contractions. address the reader as "you"
- break tasks into numbered steps — short and easy wins first
- avoid: patronizing tone, jargon, sarcasm, excessive warmth

> the user isn't reading for pleasure — they have a problem. solve it fast, then get out of their way.

---

### 4.5 release notes

**context:** new feature announcements, changelogs, app store update descriptions  
**energy level:** medium. concise and benefit-first. the merchant doesn't care what changed — they care what they can do now.

- title: under 5 words, action-verb first
- description: under 90 characters, what changed and why it matters
- body: 2 sentences max — what it is + why they should use it
- link to the feature and help center

| element | format | example |
|---|---|---|
| title | < 5 words | AI learns your catalog |
| description | < 90 characters | train your AI on product data so it recommends the right items automatically. |
| short description | 2 sentences | AI assistant now learns from your product catalog. use it to surface the right product for every customer question. |
| links | feature + help center | [try it now] [learn more] |

## 5. language rules

### 5.1 always do

- use active voice: "AI answered 80% of questions" not "80% of questions were answered"
- lead with benefit, not feature: "close more deals" not "AI-powered chat system"
- use real numbers: €1,078 · 7 days · 2,000+ merchants · 30 seconds
- write for skimming: short paragraphs, clear hierarchy, strong first sentence
- use contractions naturally: you've, it's, don't, can't
- speak to one merchant, not "merchants" in the abstract

### 5.2 never do

- overclaim without proof: not "the best AI chatbot in the world" without data
- inject humor into errors, failures, or negative states
- use corporate buzzwords: "synergy", "leverage", "empower", "unlock"
- be condescending or hand-hold excessively
- overuse "please" — save it for when you mean it
- use passive voice to soften accountability: "an error occurred" → "something went wrong — here's how to fix it"
- pepper every sentence with emojis — use them as punctuation, not decoration

### 5.3 chatty keyword vocabulary

use these consistently across all content:

| use this | not this |
|---|---|
| AI assistant | chatbot / bot / robot |
| merchant / store owner | customer (they're your merchants, not yours) |
| conversation | chat / ticket / session |
| revenue / sales | support deflection / resolution |
| data source | training data / input |
| unresolved question | failed query / unanswered ticket |
| train the AI | configure / set up the model |
| live chat | human agent / person mode |

## 6. tone spectrum — adjusting by situation

Chatty's voice is always bold and direct. our tone shifts based on what the merchant is experiencing:

| situation | merchant's emotional state | tone to use | example |
|---|---|---|---|
| exciting new feature | curious, optimistic | energetic + confident | "your AI can now recommend products based on cart behavior. set it up in 2 minutes." |
| onboarding | eager but slightly overwhelmed | warm + instructional | "let's get your AI ready. first, we'll add your store's data — it takes about 5 minutes." |
| troubleshooting / error | frustrated | calm + clear + solution-first | "something went wrong loading your data. refresh the page or contact support." |
| upsell / upgrade | weighing cost vs. value | confident + data-backed | "merchants on the growth plan see 40% more chat-attributed revenue. here's why." |
| success / milestone | proud, satisfied | brief + genuine, not over-the-top | "your AI handled 500 conversations this month. here's what's working." |

## 7. formatting & style mechanics

| rule | guidance |
|---|---|
| numbers | always write numerals for stats: 7 days, €1,078, 2,000 merchants. spell out if starting a sentence. |
| bold text | use to highlight key terms or calls to action, not for decoration. |
| links | use action verbs: [try it now] [see how it works] [read the guide] — not [click here] |
| emojis | max 1 per social post. never in documentation, errors, or formal content. |

---

## 8. quick reference card

when writing anything for Chatty, ask:

- does this sound like something a sharp, data-driven salesperson would actually say?
- is the first sentence worth reading?
- is there a number, proof point, or concrete claim?
- does it get to the point fast?
- would this land differently in an error message vs. a social post? (it should)

> if you can replace it with "this feature is powerful and helps merchants" — rewrite it. we don't do vague.
