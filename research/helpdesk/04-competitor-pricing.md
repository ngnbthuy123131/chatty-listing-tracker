# Helpdesk Niche — Competitor Pricing Analysis

## Pricing Model Comparison

| Model | Apps | Pros | Cons |
|-------|------|------|------|
| **Per-ticket** | Gorgias | Scales with volume | Unpredictable costs, penalizes growth |
| **Per-seat** | Re:amaze, Zendesk, Freshdesk, Help Scout, Front, Gladly | Predictable per agent | Expensive when scaling team |
| **Per-order** | Richpanel | Ties to business metric | Penalizes high-volume stores |
| **Flat rate** | Re:amaze Starter ($59) | Simple, predictable | May not scale |
| **Hybrid** | Richpanel (per-order + per-seat) | Flexible | Confusing |

**Unlimited seats on all plans.** All plans include: macros, rules, views, assignment, routing, CSAT survey, help center, omnichannel (email, chat, social, WhatsApp), 150+ integrations, SSO.

#### Gorgias Pricing Controversy
- **Unpredictable bills:** A store doing 500 tickets/mo on Basic ($50) pays $50 + $80 overage = **$130/mo**. During BFCM that could spike to 2,000 tickets = $50 + $680 = **$730/mo**.
- **What counts as a ticket?** Every customer-initiated conversation = 1 ticket. Auto-replies and spam can inflate counts.
- **Community complaints:** Reddit/Twitter threads regularly surface about surprise bills. Merchants feel "punished for growing."
- **AI Agent adds cost:** $0.90-$1.00 per automated resolution ON TOP of ticket costs.
- **Effective cost per ticket:** Starter = $0.20-$0.40/ticket; Basic = ~$0.17-$0.40/ticket; at scale closer to $0.15-$0.36/ticket.

## Price Sensitivity Analysis

### What Shopify Merchants Will Pay

| Merchant Size | Monthly Orders | Support Volume | Willingness to Pay |
|---------------|---------------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Starter** (0-100 orders/mo) | <100 | <50 tickets/mo | $0-$15/mo |
| **Growing** (100-500 orders/mo) | 100-500 | 50-200 tickets/mo | $15-$50/mo |
| **Established** (500-2K orders/mo) | 500-2K | 200-800 tickets/mo | $50-$150/mo |
| **Scale** (2K+ orders/mo) | 2K+ | 800+ tickets/mo | $150-$500/mo |

### Key Pricing Insights

1. **Free tier is table stakes** for adoption — Freshdesk and Help Scout offer free plans; Gorgias doesn't.
2. **Per-ticket pricing creates anxiety** — Merchants prefer predictable costs. This is Gorgias's biggest vulnerability.
3. **$29-$49/agent/mo is the sweet spot** for mid-market (Re:amaze, Freshdesk Pro).
4. **Small merchants are underserved** — The $0-$20/mo range with decent features has very few options.
5. **AI costs are additive** — Both Gorgias ($0.90/resolution) and Help Scout ($0.75/resolution) charge extra for AI automation.
6. **Chatty opportunity:** A free plan with generous limits + predictable flat-rate upgrades ($19-$49/mo) could capture merchants fleeing Gorgias pricing anxiety and those who find Zendesk/Freshdesk too generic.
