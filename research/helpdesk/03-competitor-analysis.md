# Helpdesk Niche — Competitor Analysis

## Market Overview

The Shopify helpdesk market is dominated by **Gorgias**, with Re:amaze as the strongest #2. General-purpose helpdesks (Zendesk, Freshdesk, Help Scout) have Shopify integrations but lack deep native commerce focus. A new wave of ecommerce-native tools (Richpanel, DelightChat) target the mid-market gap.

## Gorgias Deep Dive — The Dominant Player

### Key Facts
- **Founded:** 2015 (San Francisco / Paris)
- **Revenue:** ~$69M ARR (2024 estimates)
- **Status:** Shopify Premier Partner (highest tier)
- **Customers:** 15,000+ ecommerce brands
- **Funding:** $50M+ raised (Series B+)
- **Launched on Shopify:** March 2017

### Why Gorgias Wins
1. **Deepest Shopify integration** — View/edit orders, issue refunds, cancel orders, create discounts directly from the helpdesk. Customer timeline shows full purchase history.
2. **Built for ecommerce, not adapted** — Every feature is designed around commerce workflows (WISMO, returns, exchanges).
3. **Revenue attribution** — Tracks revenue generated through support interactions, giving CX teams a business case.
4. **AI Agent** — Automated resolution of common tickets (returns, order status) at $0.90-$1.00/resolution.
5. **150+ ecommerce integrations** — Klaviyo, Recharge, Loop Returns, Yotpo, etc.
6. **Omnichannel** — Email, live chat, Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, WhatsApp, SMS, Voice.

### Gorgias Weaknesses (Opportunity for Chatty)
1. **Ticket-based pricing is controversial** — Costs are unpredictable; merchants complain about surprise bills during high-volume periods (BFCM, product launches).
2. **9% 1-star reviews** — Polarized ratings (84% 5-star but 9% 1-star) suggest onboarding/pricing pain.
3. **Expensive for small merchants** — Starter at $10/mo only gets 50 tickets. Small stores with 200+ tickets/mo face $50-$300/mo bills.
4. **Complex setup** — Powerful but overwhelming for small teams.
5. **No free plan** — Only free trial, no permanently free tier.

## Re:amaze — The #2 Contender

### Key Facts
- **Owned by:** GoDaddy (acquired 2022)
- **Rating:** 4.4 ⭐ (177 reviews) — higher satisfaction than Gorgias
- **Pricing:** Per-seat ($29-$69/agent/mo) + $59 flat-rate starter plan

### Why Re:amaze is Strong
- Multi-channel (email, social, live chat, SMS, voice)
- Workflow automation, chatbots, order bots
- Live visitor view, screen sharing (Peek)
- Multi-brand management
- More predictable pricing (per-seat)
- Strong merchant satisfaction (88% 5-star)

### Re:amaze Weaknesses
- GoDaddy ownership = less startup agility
- Smaller ecosystem than Gorgias
- Less ecommerce-specific depth

## Zendesk — The Enterprise Incumbent

- **Rating:** 3.3 ⭐ (86 reviews) — worst-rated among major players
- **28% 1-star reviews** — massive dissatisfaction on Shopify
- Powerful platform but poor Shopify integration experience
- Overkill for most Shopify merchants
- Enterprise pricing ($55-$115/agent/mo for Suite)

## Who's Winning and Why

### Tier 1: Ecommerce-Native Leaders
1. **Gorgias** — Dominates through deep Shopify integration + ecommerce focus. Market leader by far.
2. **Re:amaze** — Strong #2 with better satisfaction scores but smaller footprint.

### Tier 2: General Helpdesks with Shopify Plugins
3. **Zendesk** — Big brand but terrible Shopify experience
4. **Freshdesk** — Affordable but generic
5. **Help Scout** — Beloved by SMBs but minimal ecommerce depth

### Tier 3: Niche / Emerging
6. **Richpanel** — Pivoted to self-service overlay (works ON TOP of Gorgias/Zendesk)
7. **Front** — Shared inbox, not really helpdesk
8. **Gladly / Kustomer** — Enterprise-only, not relevant for Shopify SMBs

### The Gap (Chatty's Opportunity)
- **No strong free/cheap Shopify-native helpdesk** exists
- Gorgias is too expensive for small merchants
- Zendesk/Freshdesk lack deep Shopify integration
- Re:amaze is good but not cheap ($29/agent/mo minimum)
- The $0-$30/mo Shopify-native helpdesk with good ticketing is **wide open**
