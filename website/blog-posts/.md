For years, chat in eCommerce was seen as a support win. Merchants bragged about faster response times, lower ticket volumes, and more efficient teams. Vendors reinforced this story: _install a chatbot, deflect questions, save money._

But here’s what no one said out loud: all that efficiency was hiding a massive leak.

Every month, thousands of buying-intent conversations were happening inside chat widgets, and most brands weren’t converting them into sales. They were letting revenue slip away while celebrating cost savings.

## The lightbulb moment

A few months before that moment, I had spoken with the owner of a mid-sized furniture store. He was beaming with pride.

_“We’re getting over 2,000 chat interactions per month,” he told me. “Our response time is down to 30 seconds, and we’re deflecting 85% of questions to our FAQ. The support team is finally efficient.”_

He was proud, and honestly, he had every reason to be. Those were exactly the numbers the chatbot industry had taught us to celebrate.

I congratulated him. And then, almost without thinking, I asked the obvious question:  
_“How many of those chats turn into sales?”_

Silence.

Finally, he admitted:  
_“I… we don’t really track that. Maybe 50 to 100?”_

Two thousand conversations. Fewer than 5% converted.

I didn’t say anything right away. I just sat there, nodding, but inside I was stunned. This wasn’t a success story. This was the biggest revenue leak I had ever seen.

## Digging deeper: The industry-wide problem

I wanted to determine if this was an isolated incident or part of a larger issue. So, I started digging into chats from dozens of e-commerce brands. The pattern repeated everywhere. Here’s what I saw:

-   **Customer:** “What’s the difference between the Pro and Premium versions?”
-   **Support:** “Here’s a link to our comparison chart. Anything else I can help with?”
-    **Customer:** “Thanks.” \[Leaves without buying\] 

Or:

-   **Customer:** “Will this work with my setup?”
-   **Support:** “You can check compatibility in the product specs.”
-    **Customer:** “Okay, thanks.” \[Abandons cart\]

On the surface, these were support conversations. But look closer, they were actually **buying checkpoints.**

-   “What’s the difference between Pro and Premium?” = _Help me choose which one to buy._
-   “Will this work with my setup?” = _I’m ready to buy but just need confidence it will work._

And yet the responses ended the sale instead of advancing it. This wasn’t a one-off mistake. It was an **industry-wide blind spot,** and led to:

## The realization that changed everything

That furniture retailer’s pause – the gap between “thousands of chats” and “dozens of sales” – changed everything for me.

I realized the problem wasn’t the merchants. It wasn’t even the customers. It was the **tools themselves.** Chat had been designed to serve the company, not the customer. It was optimized to save costs, not generate revenue.

But chat is purely different from any other channel.

-   Email is slow.  
    
-   Ads are passive.  
    
-   Social is noisy.

Chat is **live. Intentional. Immediate.** When someone opens that widget, they’re leaning forward. They’re ready to buy if you can just clear the last obstacle. And yet, most chats were programmed to dismiss them. That’s when…. 

## The vision becomes clear

I imagined a different kind of chat experience.

-   A customer asks about shipping. Instead of a static FAQ link, the chat agent replies:  
    _“Yes, we ship to Canada. Orders over $100 qualify for free shipping. Want me to show you our top sellers that qualify?”_
-   A customer asks about product fit. Instead of vague specs, the chat agent says:  
    _“Based on what you’ve told me, the Premium model is your best choice. Many buyers like you also grab the starter kit, it saves 15% when bundled. Should I add both to your cart?”_
-   A customer hesitates about price. Instead of backing off, the chat agent suggests:  
    _“We do have flexible payment options. Would you like me to walk you through them?”_

Same customer, same question, but two different outcomes:

-   One path ends in a closed tab.  
    
-   The other ends in a purchase.

That’s when the vision snapped into focus: chat shouldn’t be a support deflector. They should be **sales drivers.**

## The decision to build Chatty

After seeing the vision, I knew it couldn’t stay as an idea. If we believed chat should drive sales, then we had to prove it in practice.

That’s when we decided to build Chatty, an AI chatbot that could turn this vision into reality for merchants and their customers.

It was a turning point. From that day, every step of our work focused on making sales through chat possible.

## The early challenges

Of course, reimagining chat wasn’t simple. We hit three big challenges early on:

### 1\. Product intelligence

The first challenge was the AI itself.

Old AI models in the market were trained mainly on FAQs. They were good at answering questions like _“What’s your return policy?”_ but they couldn’t understand a customer’s real buying needs.

If we wanted Chatty to sell, it had to go deeper. It had to:

-   Read and understand the entire product catalog.  
    
-   Recognize the different situations customers were in.  
    
-   Recommend products and bundles with the same confidence as a trained salesperson.  
    

This was a huge step up from FAQ bots. It meant building an AI that thought in terms of value, not just answers.

### 2\. Metrics that actually matter

The second challenge was measurement.

Most teams measure chat success using metrics such as **response time** or **ticket deflection**. But none of those told us the truth we cared about: _did this chat make money?_

So we introduced new metrics:

-   **Chat-to-sales rate**: the percentage of chats that ended in a purchase.  
    
-   **Assisted revenue**: sales influenced by chat, even if the final click came later.  
    

When we first showed these numbers to merchants, many were shocked. They had never thought of chat as a revenue channel. Now they could.

### 3\. Mindset shift

The third challenge was the hardest. It wasn’t technical, it was cultural.

For years, teams were trained to move fast: answer quickly, close the ticket, and move on. However, selling requires the opposite: staying in the conversation, understanding intent, and guiding the customer toward making a purchase.

I sent [a letter to the team](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1jr8597ANDHrmNOtHc7t_KQ3TcL3rWlFEbJvbqMGcORo/edit?tab=t.0) that summed up our vision:  
**“Our job is not to close tickets. Our job is to open carts.”**

That one sentence became our compass. It reminded us every day what we were building, and why.

## The breakthrough: AI chat that sells

After months of development, mistakes, and long testing cycles, we finally reached a turning point.

Chatty began acting like a real sales associate.

-   It kept customers engaged instead of closing chats too quickly.  
    
-   It recommended specific products instead of pointing to FAQ pages.  
    
-   It asked, _“Would you like me to add this to your cart?”_ instead of, _“Anything else I can help with?”_

The first live test with a merchant gave us proof. A shopper asked about a color option. Chatty replied: _“Yes, it’s available in blue. Many customers also buy the matching tote – should I add both to your cart?”_

The shopper agreed. Two items were sold in a single conversation.

That was the moment merchants began to see the difference. Chatty wasn’t just answering questions. It was generating revenue.

## The late-night calculation (Made real)

For a long time, my “what if” math lived only in my head: _what if even 20% of those chats became sales?_

Decathlon showed us the answer in practice. With over 10,000 products, their customers asked highly technical questions: _“What size wetsuit for a 5’8” swimmer?”_ or _“Which sleeping bag works in -10°C?”_ Support teams were overwhelmed, and carts were abandoned.

When they connected their full catalog to Chatty, everything changed. The AI learned every spec, compatibility rule, and sizing chart. Within a week:

-   2,000+ conversations were handled automatically  
    
-   96.6% resolution rate was achieved  
    
-   €10k in revenue was directly attributed to AI recommendations

It wasn’t theory anymore. Real merchants, real products, real revenue. That was the moment we knew: when AI understands your catalog, **every question becomes a sales opportunity.**

-   And provides you with new metrics – **chat-to-sales, assisted** **revenue, and AOV uplift** – that demonstrate chat’s true impact.
