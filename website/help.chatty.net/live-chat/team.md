# Team

> Source: https://help.chatty.net/live-chat/team

## Availability

- Free plan: One admin seat
- Paid plan: Up to 5 team members can be invited

## What is Team?

With Chatty Team, you can invite your staff to help manage customer conversations. The platform enables collaborative support across your organization.

### Ideal Use Cases

- Multiple support staff operations
- High-volume customer service environments
- Round-the-clock shift coverage
- Scaling businesses needing expanded support capacity

## How Team Helps

- Distribute live chat management responsibilities
- Balance customer support workload
- Accelerate response times through team availability
- Monitor team performance centrally

## How Team Works

### Inviting Team Members

1. Navigate to Settings and select Manage in Teams
2. Click Invite member (Basic plan: 2 additional members; Pro plan: 4 additional members)
3. Enter member information (name and profile picture can be updated later; email cannot)
4. Click Invite to dispatch invitation email
5. Team member checks email and selects Create account
6. New member establishes Chatty account
7. Login enables conversation management

### Assigning Conversations

Manual assignment process:
1. Access Inbox
2. Select target conversation
3. Navigate to Assignee in conversation details
4. Select team member from assignment menu

### Automatic Assignment

Configure in Settings → Automation → Assignment → Automatic

Chatty automatically assigns new conversations to team members in rotation. Conversations are routed to the next available team member sequentially. This applies to conversations transferred from AI assistant features.

### Mentioning Team Members

Team members communicate via internal notes visible exclusively to staff (not customers).

**Process:**
1. Click Notes tab during conversation
2. Type message content
3. Reference team member using `@` prefix followed by name
4. Click Send

**Notifications triggered:**
- Push notification delivery
- Email notification delivery
- Access limited to conversation participants

### Replying to Notes

1. Locate target note
2. Select Reply option
3. Enter response text
4. System displays "Replying to [name]" (or "Replying to yourself" for own notes)
5. Click Send
